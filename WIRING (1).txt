RPM Meter — Wiring Reference
=============================

                          ESP32 Dev Board
                    ┌──────────────────────────┐
                    │                          │
    Encoder VCC ────┤ 3V3                 GND  ├──── Encoder GND
    Encoder GND ────┤ GND                      │
    Encoder CLK ────┤ GPIO 34 (INPUT_PULLUP)   │
    Encoder DT  ────┤ GPIO 35 (INPUT_PULLUP)   │
                    │                          │
       OLED SDA ────┤ GPIO 21 (I2C SDA)        │
       OLED SCL ────┤ GPIO 22 (I2C SCL)        │
       OLED VCC ────┤ 3V3                      │
       OLED GND ────┤ GND                      │
                    │                          │
                    └──────────────────────────┘


Component Notes
---------------

Rotary Encoder (Generic KY-040 or bare optical disc module)
  - CLK = Channel A. This is the interrupt pin. Count rising edges.
  - DT  = Channel B. Sampled at the moment of CLK rising edge
           to determine rotation direction.
  - SW  = Push-button switch (optional, not used in this project).
  - The ESP32 internal pull-up is enabled. If your encoder module
    already has 10k pull-ups on-board (KY-040 does), that is fine —
    the two pull-ups in parallel just lower the effective resistance
    slightly, which causes no harm.

OLED Display (0.96-inch SSD1306, I2C interface)
  - Default I2C address: 0x3C
  - If nothing appears, try 0x3D (solder the A0 jumper on the back
    of some modules to switch addresses).
  - Wire length: keep I2C wires under 30 cm for reliable operation.
    If you must go longer, lower the I2C clock speed in code:
      Wire.setClock(100000);  // 100 kHz instead of default 400 kHz

Power Supply
  - The ESP32 dev board is powered via USB (5V).
  - The 3V3 regulator on-board supplies both the encoder and OLED.
  - Total current draw of the project: under 80 mA, well within limits.
  - Do NOT connect 5V directly to encoder or OLED pins — both are
    3.3V logic devices.
